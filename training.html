Why should i exercise?

Besides that Working out helps you with your looks, self esteem, hormonal issues and is a solid hobby.
It also has great other great effects on physical and mental health on paper!

It can:

    (Physical-effects)

  • Increase your Muscle mass.
  • Increase strength and endurance.
  • Boost your metabolism.
  • Increase your Bone density and thus reduce risk of osteoporosis.
  • Strengthen connective tissue like tendons and ligaments, which lowers the chance of injury and prevents some suffering in the future.
  • helps with cardiovascular health by improving circulation and lowers your blood pressure.
  • Regular physical activity can reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, and certain cancers
  • a consistent workout routine supports better sleep, increases energy levels, and helps you maintain a healthy weight, contributing to a longer, healthier life.

    (Mental-effects)

  • Exercise releases Endorphins, the Body's natural Mood elevators, which help reduce stress, anxiety, and depression.
  • Emotional balancing by either acting as an Outlet of Frustrations or you could just simply enjoy yourself.
  • See for yourself what you're capable of.
  • Impress yourself even more by either becoming stronger or just gaining muscle mass (why not both?)
  • Help with building Discipline which you can use for many other Aspects in life.
  • Improve your ability to create and execute Plans.
  • Improve Self-esteem and Body image, providing a sense of accomplishment and boost confidence
  • Moreover, the routine and discipline developed through regular workouts can lead to better stress management and a more positive outlook on life. In essence, working out is not just about physical fitness; it's a powerful tool for achieving mental and emotional well-being as well.

How much and what plans?

Generally, you can base the Frequency on your current situation and just listen to your body. You have the time and want to workout every day? sure go ahead, you only have 1/2/3/ days? thats fine, all you need is a good workout plan that allows you to enjoy yourself and to train your full body (optimally twice) in seven days.
(but can differ to individual needs and its completely fine if life doesnt allow you to train everything twice).
